    Sergei Pavlovich Age, Biography

    Born on May 13, 1992, in Orlovsky, Orlovsky District, Rostov Oblast, Russia, Sergei Pavlovich is of Caucasian ethnicity and follows the Christian faith. His astrological sign is Taurus. Pavlovich commenced his training in Greco-Roman wrestling at the age of five, under the guidance of his coach, Alexander Fedorovich Aloyan.

    He pursued wrestling until the eleventh grade before joining the army. Upon his return, he began practicing army hand-to-hand combat and combat sambo.

    MMA Career

    Sergei Pavlovich made his MMA debut in 2014 within the Fight Nights Global promotion. In June 2017, he secured the Heavyweight championship with an undefeated 12-0 record, winning the title by unanimous decision over Mikhail Mokhnatkin.

    Following his success in Fight Nights Global, he signed with the UFC and debuted at UFC Fight Night 141 against Alistair Overeem, suffering his first professional loss via TKO in the first round. Subsequently, at UFC Fight Night: Overeem vs. Oleinik, he faced Marcelo Golm, winning the bout by knockout and receiving the Performance of the Night title.

    He continued his success by knocking out Maurice Greene in the first round at UFC Fight Night 162 but withdrew from his fight against Ciryl Gane in August 2020 due to an injury. He also missed his fight against Tom Aspinall at UFC Fight Night 191 in September 2021 due to visa concerns and his fight against Tanner Boser at UFC on ESPN 31 due to travel concerns.

    He achieved his second Performance of the Night award in his UFC career when he defeated Shamil Abdurakhimov via TKO in the first round at UFC Fight Night 204. In July 2022, he secured a victory over Derrick Lewis via TKO in less than a minute into the first round at UFC 277, though the outcome sparked controversy due to an early referee stoppage noticed by boxers and fans.
