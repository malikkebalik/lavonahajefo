    Suze Orman Career

    After Orman completed her education, she moved to Berkeley, California and worked as a waitress. She borrowed $52,000 from her friends and well-wishers to open a restaurant. She invested the money through a representative at Merrill Lynch where she lost the entire investment.

    Later, Orman took training as an account executive at Merrill Lynch where she worked till 1983. But to become the vice-president of investments at Prudential Bache Securities, she left Merrill. In 1987, she resigned from the Prudential and founded Suze Orman Financial Group in Emeryville, California. In 2002, she began hosting her show The Suze Orman Show on CNBC.  The show ended on March 28, 2015.

    As a writer and author, Orman wrote several books such as “The Steps to Financial Freedom”, “You Earned it Don’t Lose it”, “The Courage to be Rich”, “The Road to Wealth”, “The Laws of Money, ”The Lessons of Life” and “Women and Money”. Suze is also the former author of Yahoo!’s “Money Matters.” She contributed to The Philadelphia Inquirer and also has written for Connection Magazine.
