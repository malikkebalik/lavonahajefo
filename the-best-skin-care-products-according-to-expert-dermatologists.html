    Buyer's Guide: Find The Perfect Skin Care Products for You

    Skin care products allow you to rejuvenate your skin and keep it glowing. We can all agree upon the importance of this glow. Let's now look at the different products and how you can go about buying one for yourself!

    What Are the Different Skin Care Products?

    Skin products can be categorized by the type of skin that they are made to care for:

    • Oily
    • Sensitive Skin
    • Dry

    A lot of skin care products nowadays are compatible with all types of skin. This is great for those who have combination skin. 

    Oily skin

    Skin care products that are specifically made for oily skin are all about curbing the excessive production of sebum. This is done by ingredients like salicylic acid, glycolic acid, and benzoyl peroxide. A small amount of hyaluronic acid will provide hydration for the skin only in areas where a little moisture is needed.

    Sensitive skin

    Sensitive skin is all about reacting to stuff that doesn't normally cause a reaction. So, skin care products for sensitive skin will rarely contain any scent. They will also contain soft ingredients like aloe vera, shea butter, and chamomile extract for a soothing effect.

    Dry skin

    Dry skin, if not taken proper care of, can quickly look cracked and worn out (which is why it might even be useful to keep a callus remover at home for thicker skin). If this is the case for you, you can add a boost of radiance to your skin by simply adding a dry skin care product that contains hydrating ingredients like lactic acid, hyaluronic acid, vitamin E, and shea butter.

    What Should You Consider When Buying Skin Care Products?

    We recommend looking at these two factors when in the market for a new skin care product:

    Skin type

    Most types of skin can be classified as dry, oily, sensitive, or a combination of the three. So, the best way is to visit your dermatologist and find out this nifty detail about your skin. You can then opt for products that are good for your type of skin. 

    While the top skincare products, like the ones we took you through in our list, are good to go with all skin types, it's a good bet to go through the customer reviews and see what the buyers have to say about the "universal compatibility" of the skin product.

    Ease of use

    You must also consider the ease of using the skin care product that you're thinking of buying. If it's an ointment or serum, go through the customer reviews and see if it is easy to spread and whether the bottle is easy to open and hold. Face masks should also be easy to apply and their packaging should have instructional guidelines to help save time.

    Ingredients That You Should Avoid in Skin Care Products

    It is highly recommended that you avoid these three ingredients:

    • Sulfates
    • Fragrances
    • Parabens

    Let's now look at the harmful effects that these ingredients bring along and why they are a big NO in skin care products:

    Sulfates

    Sulfates boast cleansing properties. While they are good at cleansing, they are also good at stripping your body of its oil, which causes irritation. So, in fact, they are rather bad, with a capital B.

    Fragrances

    The purpose of adding fragrances to skin care products is rather obvious: to give it a pleasant smell. This scent can irritate sensitive noses and skin. So, it is wise to steer clear of them. Mildly scented skin care products should be okay as long as your skin isn't too sensitive.

    Parabens

    Manufacturers add parabens to their products for preservation. However, skin products containing this ingredient should be avoided at all costs, as paraben gets your hormonal levels wonky and off balance. 

    Buying Tips for the Top Skin Care Products

    These two tips will help you differentiate the best skin care products from the rest:

    The order of ingredients is important

    Did you know? The ingredient list at the back of skin care products, or any product, is in its order of concentration. So, it is fair to assume that the first five ingredients listed make up 80% of the product. 

    So, make sure that these top ingredients are good for your skin and are the ones that you are seeking in a skin care product. Any allergens or ingredients from our no-no list we just took you through should either be absent or at the bottom of the list since such a low concentration won't likely affect you.

    Versatility

    The best skin care products, like the ones we have picked for you, serve you far beyond the skin-caring part. Cleansers like the CeraVe Hydrating Facial Cleanser can even be used to remove makeup and can be applied to any part of the body.
