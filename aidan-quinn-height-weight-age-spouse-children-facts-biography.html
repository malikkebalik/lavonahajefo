    Aidan Quinn is an American-Irish actor, producer, and director, best known for the movie An Early Frost (1985), where he starred as Michael Pierson, and earned his 1st Emmy Award nomination in 1986 for “Outstanding Lead Actor in a Miniseries or a Special”. After that performance, his career skyrocketed, and roles in Stakeout (1987), Perfect Witness (1989), The Lemon Sisters (1989), and The Handmaid’s Tale (1990) followed.

    Aidan also appeared in movies such as The Playboys (1992) with Robin Wright, Benny & Joon (1993) with Johnny Depp and Julianne Moore, Legends of the Fall (1994) with Brad Pitt, Mary Shelley’s Frankenstein (1994) with Robert De Niro and Helena Bonham Carter, and Michael Collins (1996) alongside Julia Roberts and Liam Neeson. In 1997, Aidan had a lead role of Seth Warner in Commandments (1997) alongside Courteney Cox and also appeared in The Assignment the same year with Ben Kingsley. He produced 1 episode of The Book of Daniels in 2006 and also starred in the same TV series in 2016 for 8 episodes. Aidan was credited as a director of 1 episode of Elementary TV series in 2016, in which he also starred from 2012 to 2018 as Captain Thomas Gregson.

    Aidan Quinn Facts

  • He was born on March 8, which became International Women’s Day in 1910.
  • Aiden had an early start as an actor in Chicago theatre when he was 19 years old and later trained at the Piven Theatre Workshop in Evanston, Illinois, United States.
  • His daughter, Ava Eileen Quinn, suffers from autism, and Aidan claimed the vaccine she received when she was a toddler was to blame.
  • Due to his daughter’s illness, he became a major supporter of the International Children’s Media Center (ICMC), The American Ireland Fund (AIF), The Autism Coalition, and National Alliance for Autism Research (NAAR) charities, and spoke at 2003 Night of Too Many Stars, a fundraising charity event for children with autism.
  • Aidan also supported a campaign raising awareness of testicular cancer titled One For The Boys, organized and promoted by Samuel L. Jackson.
  • Ava, his daughter, appeared alongside Aiden in a movie Avalon (1990), where she played the baby David, while his other daughter, Eva, played a ghost in The Eclipse in 2009.
  • He won the IFTA Award for “Best Actor in a Supporting Role in a Film” in 2010 for his role in The Eclipse (2009).
  • He said that he doesn’t care about being famous and that he accepts roles that he likes, instead of ones that would benefit his career the most.
  • Aidan isn’t a big fan of cities, and prefers traveling and leading a nomad life. He visited a remote island off the coast of Kenya at one point and likes running and hiking through the woods.
  • He cites starring in Songcatcher (2000) movie was one of the best experiences of his life, as it let him play guitar at night after the shooting was finished, as well as drink wine, sing to himself, and smoke a cigar.
  • Aidan shared a Special Jury Prize at the Sundance Film Festival in 2000 with his co-stars in Songcatcher (2000).
  • Aidan claimed that Madonna was a great artist and had a terrific personality after they filmed a movie together in 1985, titled Desperately Seeking Susan.
  • 21 years passed between Aiden’s 2 Emmy Award nominations. After the 1st one for An Early Frost in 1985, where he had a lead role, he was nominated for the supporting role of Senator Dawes in Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee (2007) but didn’t win.
  • He had a notable role in the drama film Stay in 2013, alongside Taylor Schilling.
  • Aidan portrayed Paul McCartney, a well-known musician, and member of The Beatles, in a television drama by VH1 titled Two of Us (2000). He had the chance to meet Paul later in life, and the two became good friends.
  • He co-starred with Sandra Bullock, Nicole Kidman, and Evan Rachel Wood in a fantasy-drama Practical Magic (1998).
  • He became good friends with Natasha Richardson, Liam Neeson’s ex-wife, during the movie they starred in together – The Handmaid’s Tale (1990), and she made him a co-executor of real estate in her will in 2005. Natasha passed away in 2009 after she fell and received a fatal head injury while she was skiing in Mont Tremblant, Quebec, Canada.
  • He is not active on social media.
